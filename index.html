Welcome to the World of Oryx and Crake

A Glimpse Into a Dystopian Future by Margaret Atwood

Themes

Genetic Engineering & Bioethics

Explore creations like the pigoons and Crakers, symbolizing humanity's pursuit of god-like control.

“What is it about this planet that is so insistently homicidal?”

Environmental Collapse

Society's exploitation of nature and consumerism lead to catastrophe in Atwood's world.

Loneliness & Love

Jimmy's internal struggle reflects the human need for connection in a world without compassion.

Characters

Jimmy (Snowman)

A survivor and storyteller, caught between the past and a post-human future.

“You can't buy it, but it has a price.”

Crake

The genius architect of a new species, Crake sees himself as the savior of humanity through science.

“Nature is to zoos as God is to churches.”

Oryx

A symbol of innocence and survival, Oryx’s mysterious past shapes her relationships with both Crake and Jimmy.

Context

Real-World Inspirations

Atwood drew from modern science—like CRISPR—and corporate overreach to imagine her dystopia.

Author’s Note

Margaret Atwood has said, "Nothing in this book is beyond current science." The story serves as a cautionary tale.

What Happens Next?

The ending leaves us asking: Can the Crakers survive without guidance? Is Snowman the last human?

You decide.
